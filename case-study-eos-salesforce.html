Salesforce B2B Commerce Enterprise UX Design System Capita PLC

Evolution of Sales (EOS) B2B Commerce Platform

Architected an end-to-end B2B commerce experience on Salesforce, transforming manual quoting into an automated storefront and projecting a 52% uplift in product sales revenue.

Timeline
2024 – Present
My Role
Lead UX Architect
Platform
Salesforce B2B Commerce
Client
Fire Service College

Context

The Challenge

Capita's Fire Service College needed to modernize their course booking and product sales process. The existing system relied on manual quoting, phone calls, and spreadsheets—creating friction for both customers and internal teams.

The business goal was clear: transform this into a self-service digital storefront while maintaining the personalized service that their B2B customers expected.

Key Problem Statement: How might we create a B2B commerce experience that handles complex course configurations while feeling as simple as a B2C shopping experience?

Discovery

Understanding the Problem Space

1

Stakeholder Interviews

Conducted sessions with sales teams, operations, and leadership to understand pain points and business objectives.

2

Competitive Analysis

Evaluated 5+ B2B commerce platforms to identify best practices for complex product configuration.

3

User Research

Interviewed training coordinators from fire services to understand their booking needs and workflows.

4

Platform Assessment

Analyzed Salesforce B2B Commerce capabilities and limitations to inform design decisions.

Key Findings

  • Complex pricing: Each course had different pricing based on delegates, dates, and accommodation options
  • Bulk bookings: Training coordinators often book 5-20 delegates at once
  • Terms acceptance: Legal requirements mandated terms acceptance before cart addition
  • Return visits: Most users returned to book again, making account features critical

Solution

Designing the Experience

1. Product Discovery & Catalogue

Created a visually-rich storefront that showcases courses by category, with hero imagery and clear value propositions.

Homepage featuring top-selling courses, promotions, and categorical browsing

2. Course Configuration & Booking

The product detail page handles complex configuration while maintaining clarity. Users can select dates, number of delegates, and accommodation—with real-time pricing updates.

Course detail page with inline configuration, terms acceptance, and suggested products

3. Cart & Checkout

The cart experience allows easy modification of quantities and provides clear pricing breakdown including discounts.

Cart with quantity controls, pricing breakdown, and checkout flow

Design Decisions

  • Dark theme: Aligned with Fire Service College branding
  • Inline configuration: All course options visible on one screen
  • Progressive disclosure: Accommodation options appear when checkbox selected
  • Sticky pricing: Total price always visible during configuration

Reflection

What I Learned

  • Platform constraints can drive creativity. Salesforce B2B Commerce has limitations, but working within them forced us to prioritize ruthlessly.
  • B2B doesn't mean boring. Professional users still appreciate beautiful, intuitive interfaces.
  • Legal requirements need UX thinking. The terms acceptance requirement could have been a friction point, but integrating it thoughtfully made it nearly invisible.
