Context

The Challenge

Capita's marketing team was eager to leverage AI image generation tools like Midjourney and DALL-E for campaigns, but faced significant challenges:

  • Brand inconsistency ‚Äî Different team members produced wildly different visual styles
  • Prompt confusion ‚Äî Writing effective prompts required significant trial and error
  • Quality control ‚Äî No clear guidelines on what made an AI image "on-brand"
  • Time waste ‚Äî Hours spent iterating on prompts with unpredictable results
  • Legal concerns ‚Äî Uncertainty about copyright and usage rights

Key Problem Statement: How might we create a system that enables anyone on the marketing team to generate professional, brand-consistent AI imagery without being a prompt engineering expert?

Discovery

Research & Analysis

I conducted workshops with the marketing and creative teams to understand their needs, studied AI art generation best practices, and analyzed what made imagery feel "on-brand" for Capita.

Key Findings

  • 80% of prompts were being rewritten 5+ times before getting usable results
  • No shared vocabulary ‚Äî Team members used inconsistent terminology for styles
  • Corporate photography gaps ‚Äî Stock photos felt generic, but AI offered potential
  • Fear of the technology ‚Äî Some team members avoided AI tools entirely

Reflection

What I Learned

  • Constraints enable creativity. Counter-intuitively, the style presets and templates didn't limit creativity‚Äîthey freed team members to focus on content rather than wrestling with tools.
  • AI amplifies existing skills. The best results came from team members who understood brand principles. AI is a tool, not a replacement for creative judgment.
  • Documentation is essential. Without clear guidelines, AI-generated imagery quickly becomes chaotic. The style guide and checklist were as valuable as the templates themselves.
  • Ethics require attention. We built in diversity requirements and prohibited certain imagery types to ensure responsible AI use.
