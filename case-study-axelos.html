The Challenge

Axelos is a joint venture between the UK Government and Capita, responsible for managing globally recognized best practice frameworks including ITIL¬Æ and PRINCE2¬Æ. The legacy platform was struggling with accessibility compliance and user engagement across diverse international markets.

With over 500,000 users relying on the platform for certification pathways, any redesign needed to maintain feature parity while dramatically improving usability and meeting strict GDS accessibility standards.

Key Problem Statement: How might we transform a complex certification platform to meet UK Government accessibility standards while improving engagement across 8 international markets?

Design Decisions for Accessibility

  • Color contrast: All text meets WCAG AA 4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum
  • Focus indicators: Clear, visible focus states on all interactive elements
  • Form accessibility: Proper labels, error messages, and validation for all forms
  • Responsive design: Touch targets of 44px minimum on mobile
  • Skip links: Allow keyboard users to bypass repetitive navigation
  • ARIA landmarks: Semantic structure for screen reader navigation

Reflection

What I Learned

  • Accessibility is not a feature‚Äîit's a foundation. Building accessibility into the design process from day one is far more efficient than retrofitting.
  • Real user testing is irreplaceable. Automated tools catch technical issues, but testing with actual users with disabilities reveals the true experience.
  • Government standards drive quality. Working to GDS standards elevated the overall design quality beyond just accessibility compliance.
  • International requires cultural sensitivity. Supporting 8 markets meant considering not just translation but cultural expectations and reading patterns.
