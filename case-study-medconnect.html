The Challenge

CareBridge Health Network, a network of 12 hospitals across the Midwest US, had invested significantly in a patient portal‚Äîbut only 8% of patients were using it. The majority, especially those 55 and older, preferred calling the hospital for appointments, prescription refills, and test results, overwhelming staff and creating long wait times.

Harman Connected Services was engaged to completely redesign the portal experience with a focus on accessibility, trust, and ease of use for an aging patient population.

HIPAA Compliant Design

Key Problem Statement: How might we create a patient portal that elderly and less tech-savvy users can confidently use to manage their healthcare‚Äîreducing call center burden while improving patient outcomes?

Design

Accessibility-First Approach

I redesigned every component with accessibility as the primary constraint, not an afterthought. Here's how we transformed key elements:

‚ùå Before

Small, Low-Contrast Text

  • 12px font size throughout
  • Gray text on white (#777 on #fff)
  • 2.5:1 contrast ratio (fails AA)
  • No text resize option

‚úì After

Large, High-Contrast Text

  • 18px minimum font size
  • Dark text on white (#333 on #fff)
  • 7:1 contrast ratio (exceeds AAA)
  • 3-step text resize control

‚ùå Before

Tiny Touch Targets

  • 24px button height
  • Buttons close together
  • No focus indicators
  • Mouse-only interactions

‚úì After

Large Touch Targets

  • 48px minimum touch target
  • 24px spacing between actions
  • Visible focus rings (3px)
  • Full keyboard navigation

‚ùå Before

Inaccessible Forms

  • Placeholder-only labels
  • No error descriptions
  • Color-only error indication
  • Time-limited sessions (2 min)

‚úì After

Fully Accessible Forms

  • Persistent, visible labels
  • Descriptive error messages
  • Icon + color + text for errors
  • Extended timeout with warning

Reflection

What I Learned

  • Accessibility benefits everyone. Features designed for elderly users (larger text, clearer buttons, simpler navigation) improved satisfaction scores across ALL age groups.
  • Test with real assistive technology users. Automated accessibility tools catch only 30% of issues. Real users with real disabilities revealed critical problems we never would have found otherwise.
  • Healthcare requires extra trust signals. Users needed constant reassurance that their data was secure. We added HIPAA badges, encryption indicators, and clear privacy messaging throughout.
  • Reduce cognitive load for stressed users. Patients accessing health information are often anxious. Every unnecessary step or confusing element amplifies that anxiety.
